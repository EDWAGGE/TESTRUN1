About

Artificial intelligence is becoming a foundational technology in modern financial systems. Advances in data availability, computing power, and machine learning techniques are enabling new applications across payments, lending, investment management, supervision, and risk assessment.

This platform provides a global, development-focused perspective on AI in finance, examining both its transformative potential and the policy challenges it presents for regulators, financial institutions, and development partners.

Key Themes

Efficiency and Innovation

AI-driven automation and analytics are reducing costs, improving service delivery, and enabling faster, more informed financial decision-making across institutions.

Risk Management and Stability

Machine learning models enhance credit assessment, fraud detection, and market surveillance, while also introducing new forms of model and systemic risk.

Financial Inclusion

By leveraging alternative data and digital channels, AI can expand access to financial services for underserved households and small enterprises.

Ethics and Trust

Issues of bias, transparency, explainability, and data privacy are central to ensuring AI systems are fair, accountable, and trusted by users.

Policy and Governance Considerations

Policymakers face the challenge of enabling innovation while safeguarding financial stability, consumer protection, and market integrity. Effective governance frameworks emphasize proportional regulation, transparency, accountability, and international coordination.

Tools such as regulatory sandboxes, AI audits, supervisory technology, and capacity-building initiatives play a critical role in supporting responsible adoption across diverse financial systems.
